About KDA School

KDA School is a community-focused institution committed to academic excellence, practical skills, and character development. We serve students from primary through secondary levels and offer a balanced curriculum combining STEM, arts, sports, and community service. Our mission is to produce curious, capable, and compassionate graduates who contribute positively to society.

Recent Achievements

  • National Coding Challenge — 1st Place: KDA students won the national inter-school coding tournament with an AI-driven attendance assistant.
  • Green Campus Certification: Solar panels and a rainwater harvesting system reduced campus energy usage by 34%.
  • Community Health Drive: Over 2,000 residents served during the annual health outreach led by the school's volunteer corps.
  • Art & Culture Festival: Student performances attracted visitors from across the region and raised funds for library expansion.

Why These Achievements Matter

Achievements are not only trophies or certificates; they reflect a combination of strong leadership, supportive families, and engaged students. Each success fuels further improvement — attracting better teachers, more resources, and partnerships with organizations that provide internships and scholarships for KDA students.

Customer & Employee (Community) Interest

We value both our customers (families, alumni, donors) and employees (teachers, staff) equally. Here’s how we keep them engaged and informed:

  • Regular Newsletters: Monthly updates highlighting student achievements, calendar events, and opportunities to support projects.
  • Open Days & Workshops: Prospective families and community partners visit the campus to see demonstrations and student showcases.
  • Staff Professional Development: Continuous training enables teachers to adopt modern teaching practices and digital tools.
  • Feedback Channels: Surveys, suggestion boxes, and parent-teacher forums ensure voices are heard and used to refine programs.

Note: "Customer interest" here refers to parents, guardians, alumni and local partners who support and evaluate the school. Employee interest refers to staff engagement, retention, and professional growth.
